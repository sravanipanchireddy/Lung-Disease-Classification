Pneumonia
Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs. The air sacs may fill with fluid or pus (purulent material), causing cough with phlegm or pus, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing.

Covid-19
COVID-19 is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It primarily spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. Symptoms include fever, cough, shortness of breath, fatigue, body aches, loss of taste or smell, sore throat, and diarrhea.

Tuberculosis
Tuberculosis (TB) is a bacterial infection that primarily affects the lungs but can also affect other parts of the body. It spreads through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes. Symptoms include coughing that lasts three or more weeks, coughing up blood, chest pain, unintentional weight loss, fatigue, fever, and night sweats.

About Lung Disease Prediction

Lung diseases, including COVID-19, tuberculosis (TB), pneumonia, and healthy lung function, present formidable global health challenges, necessitating early detection and precise diagnosis for effective intervention. The SARS-CoV-2 virus, responsible for COVID-19, sparked a pandemic in late 2019, showcasing varying degrees of severity, from mild flu-like symptoms to severe pneumonia and fatality, underscoring the urgency for accurate identification and treatment. TB, caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis, remains a significant health threat, particularly in impoverished regions, primarily affecting the lungs. Pneumonia, an inflammatory lung disease often triggered by pathogens, poses risks, especially to vulnerable demographics like young children and the elderly. Understanding these conditions' nuances is paramount for tailored management and prevention strategies. In the realm of medical imaging, convolutional neural networks (CNNs) are instrumental for lung disease classification, adept at learning hierarchical representations of image features, aiding in accurate diagnosis. Moreover, recurrent neural networks (RNNs), such as Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks and their bidirectional variants (BiLSTM), are crucial for analyzing sequential medical imaging data like chest X-rays (CXRs), capturing temporal dependencies for enhanced diagnostic accuracy. These advancements in deep learning hold transformative potential, promising to revolutionize clinical practice by providing trustworthy models for precise diagnosis, treatment planning, and ultimately improving public health outcomes globally.
